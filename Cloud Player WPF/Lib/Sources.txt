Third party libraries:
Awesomium, http://www.awesomium.com/ (webbrowser, used to display online videos; chosen because it is a popular opensource solution)
Windows Media Player (used to play local videos; used because it was simplest to get working, VLC was a pain that I ultimatly didn´t get to work; directshow had some issues with focus: the rendered video was a seperate window and app lost focus)
Microsoft.WindowsAPICodePack (brings functions to take advantage of new features in Windows 7, used to determine length of local videos and to show progress in taskbar on splash screen)
Google.Apis.YouTube.v3 (a .net wrapper for the YouTube data api)
ObjectListView, http://objectlistview.sourceforge.net/cs/index.html (an wrapper control around System.Windows.Forms.ListView that makes working with it easier)
